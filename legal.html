3. Options Trading Risk Disclosure

‚ö†Ô∏è Options Are High-Risk Securities

Options trading involves substantial risk of loss and is not appropriate for all investors. You could lose your entire investment in a very short period of time.

Before using this Service, you should understand the following about options trading:

  • Options are leveraged instruments. A relatively small market movement can result in proportionately much larger gains or losses.
  • The buyer of an option may lose the entire premium paid for that option if it expires out of the money.
  • The seller (writer) of an option may face theoretically unlimited losses on naked positions.
  • Credit spreads limit loss to the width of the spread minus premium received, but losses can still be substantial relative to account size.
  • Options contracts have expiration dates. Time decay (theta) works against option buyers and can rapidly erode the value of positions.
  • Implied volatility changes can significantly impact option prices independent of the underlying stock's movement.
  • Early assignment risk exists for all short option positions and may result in unexpected stock positions or margin calls.

You should read the Options Clearing Corporation's disclosure document "Characteristics and Risks of Standardized Options" before trading options. This document is available from your broker or at theocc.com.
